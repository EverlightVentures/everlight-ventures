## 1. AMAZON AUTHOR CENTRAL

### Why This Matters

Author Central is the control panel for how your brand appears on Amazon. Every book listing links back to this page. It is the first thing a parent or teacher sees when they click your author name. A well-built Author Central page converts browsers into series buyers.

### Setting Up Everlight Kids as an Author Brand

**Step 1: Create the Account**

1. Go to author.amazon.com
2. Sign in with the same Amazon account used for KDP
3. Search for your published book title to claim authorship
4. Author name should match EXACTLY what is on KDP: the name you publish under (either your real name or "Everlight Kids" if publishing under the brand name)

**Decision Point -- Author Name Strategy:**

| Option | Pros | Cons | Recommendation |
|--------|------|------|----------------|
| Your real name | Personal brand, credibility, can write other genres | Less scalable, tied to one person | Use if you want personal recognition |
| Everlight Kids | Scalable brand, can add co-authors later, professional publisher feel | Less personal, harder for school visits | Use if building toward a publishing imprint |
| Real name + "Everlight Kids" | Best of both ("Jane Doe writing as Everlight Kids") | Slightly confusing | Good compromise for now |

**Recommendation:** Publish under your real name with "Everlight Kids" as the publisher/imprint. This lets you build personal author credibility while keeping the brand scalable. Author Central page is under your real name; publisher line on the books says "Everlight Kids -- A division of Everlight Ventures."

**Step 2: Write the Author Bio**

The bio should speak to TWO audiences: parents browsing Amazon and educators evaluating the books for classroom use. Keep it warm, credible, and focused on the mission.

**Template Author Bio (customize with your real details):**

```
[Author Name] is the creator of the Adventures with Sam and Robo series, published
under the Everlight Kids imprint. The series was born from a simple idea: what if a
children's book could teach phonics, science, emotional intelligence, and values -- all
inside the same adventure?

Each book in the series follows Sam and his robot best friend Robo as they discover
new superpowers through curiosity, kindness, and hard work. The stories integrate
Science of Reading phonics instruction (CVC patterns, compound words, blends,
digraphs, and long vowels) directly into the narrative, making learning
indistinguishable from fun.

Every book is also an interactive coloring book -- with full-color illustrations on
one page and the same scene in black-and-white on the facing page. Two books in one.

The series is aligned to Common Core State Standards (CCSS) for grades PreK-2 and
maps to all five CASEL competencies for social-emotional learning. Each book includes
circle time discussion prompts, an educator note, and a back matter learning section.

[Author Name] believes that every word is a door and every door is an adventure.
When not writing, [he/she/they] can be found [personal detail -- e.g., exploring
nature trails, building Lego robots, or reading aloud to neighborhood kids].

For educator resources, free coloring pages, and classroom guides, visit
everlightkids.com.
```

**Character count:** Stay under 2,000 characters. Amazon truncates longer bios.

**Step 3: Author Page Customization**

| Element | Action | Priority |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Author photo | Professional headshot OR Everlight Kids logo. If using logo, make it high-res (minimum 300x300 px). A personal photo converts better for children's books -- parents want to know who wrote the book their kid is reading. | HIGH |
| Author bio | Use the template above, customized | HIGH |
| Blog feed | Connect your blog (everlightkids.com) or leave blank. Do NOT connect a dead blog. | MEDIUM |
| Videos | Upload a 60-90 second "Meet the Author" video. Film on your phone. Show the books, flip through pages, talk about why you wrote the series. This converts. | HIGH |
| Events | Add any planned read-aloud events, school visits, or book fairs | LOW (add when scheduled) |
| Editorial reviews | Add any professional reviews (Kirkus, SLJ, Booklist) as they come in | HIGH (when available) |

**Step 4: Link All 5 Books to the Author Page**

1. In Author Central, go to the "Books" tab
2. Click "Add more books"
3. Search for each title by name or ISBN
4. Claim all 5 individual books + the box set
5. Verify each listing appears correctly on your author page
6. If a book does not appear, it may take 5-7 business days after KDP publication for Author Central to recognize it

**Step 5: Editorial Reviews Section**

Amazon allows you to add editorial reviews to each book listing through Author Central. These appear near the top of the product page and carry significant weight.

**Sources for editorial reviews:**

| Source | How to Get It | Cost | Timeline |

| Kirkus Reviews (Indie) | Submit at kirkusreviews.com/indie | $450/review | 7-9 weeks |

1. Go to Author Central > Books > select the book
2. Click "Editorial Reviews"
3. Add the review text and attribute it to the source (e.g., "Kirkus Reviews")
4. These reviews appear on the Amazon product page in the "Editorial Reviews" section

## 2. AMAZON A+ CONTENT (BRAND REGISTRY)

### Understanding A+ Content for Books

Amazon A+ Content (formerly Enhanced Brand Content) lets you add rich media -- images, comparison charts, infographics, and formatted text -- below the standard product description on your listing. For children's books, this is where you SHOW the interior format, the coloring pages, the phonics progression, and the series arc visually.

**Important distinction for KDP authors:** A+ Content for books is available through KDP's A+ Content manager. You do NOT need Amazon Brand Registry (which is for physical product sellers in Seller Central). KDP has its own A+ Content system with 17 available modules.

To access: Log into KDP > go to Marketing tab > A+ Content

### Recommended A+ Content Modules for Children's Books

Build your A+ Content using the following modules. Apply the SAME A+ Content to all 5 books (with minor per-book customizations) for brand consistency.

#### Module 1: Series Overview Banner (Full-Width Image with Text)

**Module type:** Standard Image and Light Text Overlay

**Design specs:**
- Dimensions: 970 x 600 px (full width)
- Show all 5 book covers arranged left to right in order
- Tagline text overlay: "Every word is a door. And every door is an adventure."
- Below covers: one-line summary per book
  - Book 1: Understanding Animals | CVC Phonics
  - Book 2: Scientific Tasks | Compound Words
  - Book 3: Heightened Senses | Blends and Digraphs
  - Book 4: Communicating with Nature | Long Vowels
  - Book 5: Leadership | Fluency Mastery
- Background: soft gradient using the series color palette (gold to green to blue)

**Where to design:** Canva (free tier works). Use 970px wide canvas. Export as PNG.

**Cost:** $0 (Canva free) or $13/month (Canva Pro for brand kit features)

#### Module 2: Dual-Format Showcase (Side-by-Side Image)

**Module type:** Standard Comparison Chart OR Standard Image and Dark Text Overlay

**Design specs:**
- Show a two-panel image:
  - LEFT panel: A B&W coloring page from the book interior (scan or export from your illustration file)
  - RIGHT panel: The same scene in full color
  - Dividing line in the middle with text: "Two books in one."
- Caption text: "Every left page is a coloring illustration. Every right page is the same scene in full color. Story time meets coloring time."

**Why this matters:** This is the single most differentiating feature of the series. Many buyers will not understand the coloring hybrid format from the description alone. SHOWING it visually closes the sale.

#### Module 3: Phonics Progression Chart (Standard Comparison Chart)

**Module type:** Standard Comparison Chart

**Design as a visual infographic showing the 5-step phonics ladder:**

**Arrow graphic** connecting each step to the next, showing progression from left to right.

**Tagline below chart:** "A complete early reading journey -- from first sounds to full fluency."

#### Module 4: Educational Credentials Block (Standard Image and Text)

**Module type:** Standard Image and Light Text Overlay

**Content:**

  - Includes Educator Notes and Activity Suggestions
- If/when available: pull a one-line quote from a teacher or librarian review

**Why:** Parents trust institutional credibility signals. Teachers search for standards-aligned materials. This module targets both.

#### Module 5: Cross-Sell Comparison Chart (All 5 Books)

**Module type:** Standard Comparison Chart

**Columns:** One per book (5 columns)

| Feature | Book 1 | Book 2 | Book 3 | Book 4 | Book 5 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Title | Sam's First Superpower | Sam's Second Superpower | Sam's Third Superpower | Sam's Fourth Superpower | Sam's Fifth Superpower |

**Each column links to that book's product page.** This is the most powerful cross-sell tool on Amazon.

#### Module 6: "What's Inside" Feature Showcase

**Module type:** Standard Three Images and Text

**Three panels:**

1. **Panel 1 -- "Read"**: Image of a color illustration spread. Text: "Beautifully illustrated stories that teach phonics through adventure, not worksheets."

2. **Panel 2 -- "Color"**: Image of a B&W coloring page. Text: "Every story scene has a matching coloring page. Kids engage with the story through art."

### A+ Content Best Practices for Children's Books

1. **Use images, not walls of text.** Parents scanning children's book pages are visual buyers. Every module should be image-heavy.
2. **Show the interior.** The number one conversion driver for illustrated children's books is showing what the inside looks like. Parents want to see the art quality and format before buying.
3. **Repeat the series message.** Every A+ module should reinforce: "5 books, progressive phonics, coloring hybrid, values-driven." Repetition builds trust.
4. **Mobile-first design.** Over 60% of Amazon browsing is on mobile. Design images at 970px wide but test that text is readable on a phone screen. Minimum font size in images: 16pt.
5. **Update A+ Content when you add new books.** Every time a new book launches, update the comparison chart and series banner to include it.

## 3. SERIES PAGE OPTIMIZATION

### How to Properly Link All 5 Books as a Series on Amazon

Amazon's series page is a dedicated page that shows all books in a series, in order, with format options. When set up correctly, each individual book page shows a "This is book X of Y in the [Series Name]" link that drives readers to explore the full series.

**Step-by-Step Setup (via KDP Dashboard):**

1. Log into kdp.amazon.com
2. Go to your Bookshelf
3. Click the "+ Create new title or series" button
4. Select "Create series page"
5. Series name: **Adventures with Sam and Robo**
6. Add each book in order:
   - Book 1: Sam's First Superpower
   - Book 2: Sam's Second Superpower
   - Book 3: Sam's Third Superpower
   - Book 4: Sam's Fourth Superpower
   - Book 5: Sam's Fifth Superpower
7. For each book, assign the correct series number (1-5)
8. Do NOT add the box set to the main series numbering -- it should be listed separately or as an unnumbered entry

**Alternative method (for already-published books):**
1. Go to Bookshelf
2. Click the "..." (ellipsis) next to a published book
3. Click "Add to series"
4. Select your series or create a new one
5. Assign the series number
6. Repeat for each book

**Timeline:** The series page goes live within 72 hours of all titles being published and linked.

### Series Metadata Consistency

Every book MUST have matching metadata for the series page to work correctly:

| Field | Must Match Across All Books |
|-------|-----------------------------|
| Series name | "Adventures with Sam and Robo" (exact match, no variations) |
| Author name | Identical on all 5 books |
| Publisher name | "Everlight Kids" on all 5 books |
| Age range | 3-8 on all 5 books |
| Grade range | PreK-2 on all 5 books |

**Common mistake:** Using slightly different series names (e.g., "Adventures with Sam & Robo" vs. "Adventures with Sam and Robo"). The ampersand vs. "and" mismatch will break the series linking. Pick one and use it everywhere.

### Series Page URL

Once live, your series page URL will be: `amazon.com/dp/B0XXXXXXXXX` (Amazon assigns a series ASIN). Bookmark this URL. Use it in all marketing materials, email signatures, and social media bios. This single link shows ALL books and formats in one view.

**To find your series page URL:**
1. Go to KDP > Marketing > Series Pages
2. Click "View on Amazon" next to your series
3. Copy the URL from your browser

### Box Set Positioning on the Series Page

| Product | Series Number | Position |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Book 1 | 1 | First in series |
| Book 2 | 2 | Second in series |
| Book 3 | 3 | Third in series |
| Book 4 | 4 | Fourth in series |
| Book 5 | 5 | Fifth in series |
| Box Set (Ebook) | Leave blank | Appears as "Related" or separate listing |
| Box Set (Paperback) | Leave blank | Appears as "Related" or separate listing |

**Do NOT number the box set as "6"** -- this confuses buyers into thinking there are 6 books in the series. The box set should be a standalone listing that cross-references the series in its description and keywords.

### "Complete the Series" Widget Optimization

Amazon automatically generates a "Complete the Series" prompt for customers who have purchased some books but not all. To maximize this:

1. **Consistent pricing across all books.** If Book 3 is randomly $4.99 while the others are $2.99, it creates friction. Keep all individual book prices identical.
2. **Link all formats.** Make sure the Kindle, paperback, and hardcover editions of each book are properly linked on the same product page. This happens automatically if you publish all formats under the same KDP title, but verify by checking each book's product page.
3. **Back matter cross-sell.** At the end of every book, include a "Continue Sam's Adventure" page with the title and a one-line teaser for the next book. Include the text "Available now on Amazon" and the series name.

## 5. REVIEW AND SOCIAL PROOF STRATEGY

### Why Reviews Matter More for Children's Books

Parents are risk-averse buyers. They will not hand a book to their child without social proof. For children's books, the minimum credibility threshold is:
- **10 reviews** before most parents will consider purchasing
- **25 reviews** before Amazon's algorithm treats the book as established
- **50+ reviews** to compete with established children's series

### Amazon Vine Program -- Eligibility Assessment

**Current status for KDP authors:** Amazon Vine is primarily designed for FBA physical product sellers. To use Vine for books, you would need to:

1. Print physical copies of the book (outside of KDP print-on-demand)
2. Ship those copies to Amazon's FBA fulfillment center
3. Enroll those FBA-listed books in the Vine program
4. Cost: Vine enrollment fee ($200 per parent ASIN as of 2025) + printing and shipping costs

**Recommendation for a solo publisher: Skip Vine.** The cost and complexity of creating a separate FBA listing for a book you already sell via KDP is not worth it when there are easier, free methods to get reviews. Vine makes sense for physical products with high margins, not $9.99 paperbacks.

### ARC (Advance Reader Copy) Strategy

ARCs are the most reliable way to generate Day 1 reviews. Here is a step-by-step plan.

**Step 1: Build an ARC list (start 8 weeks before Book 1 launch)**

Target 20-30 ARC readers. Sources:

| Source | How to Find Them | Expected Response Rate |
|--------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Parenting book bloggers | Search "children's book review blog" + contact via blog email | 10-15% |
| BookTok/Bookstagram parents | Search #kidsbooks #picturebooks on TikTok/Instagram, DM creators with 1K-10K followers | 5-10% |
| Homeschool communities | Post in homeschool Facebook groups (The Homeschool Momma, Homeschool Buyers Co-op) | 15-20% |
| Teacher influencers | Search "teacher book reviews" on Instagram, contact via DM | 10-15% |
| Goodreads groups | Join "Children's Book Reviews" and "Picture Book Lovers" groups. Post ARC offer. | 20-30% |

### "Look Inside" Optimization

2. **Include the coloring page format in the preview.** The B&W/color dual-page format MUST be visible in "Look Inside." 3. **Back matter preview.** "Look Inside" also shows the last 10%. Include the Learning Corner, Values Moment, and the "Continue Sam's Adventure" cross-sell page in the final pages so previewing parents see the educational depth.
