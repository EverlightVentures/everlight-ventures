# Beyond the Veil -- Voice Brand SOP
## The Keith Morrison Standard

**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-02-28
**Project:** *Bey8ne the Viel (Beyond the Veil)*
**Purpose:** Define and lock the sonic identity of this IP so every narrator audition, social clip, and ad creative stays on-brand.

---

## 1. The Sonic Identity

This book's voice is **gravitas + suspense + intimacy**. Think Keith Morrison on Dateline: slow, deliberate, almost conspiratorial -- like he's sharing a secret with one person, not broadcasting to millions.

### Core Voice Traits

| Trait | Description | What to Avoid |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Texture** | Gravelly baritone. Lived-in. Slightly raspy. | Smooth, polished, radio-bright voices |
| **Pacing** | Slow-to-medium. Lets silence breathe. | Rushing. Filling every gap. |
| **Inflection** | Rising at key moments for tension. Drawling down to land a point. | Monotone OR over-the-top performance |
| **Energy Level** | Low-burn intensity. Not loud. The quiet that makes you lean in. | Explosive highs, yelling, over-emoting |
| **Intimacy** | Read to ONE person in the room. | Projecting like a stage performance |
| **Accent** | Neutral American with hint of Southern draw acceptable | Heavy regional or foreign accents |

## 2. Narrator Audition Script (Test Passage)

Use this exact passage for all auditions. It stress-tests pacing, tension, and character differentiation.

---

**PASSAGE A -- Narrative Voice (Chapter opener style):**

> "Nobody saw it coming. That's what they always say, isn't it? That the danger was right there -- in plain sight -- and yet somehow, impossibly, no one noticed. But the truth is more complicated than that. The truth usually is."

What to listen for: Does their silence between sentences feel intentional or nervous? Do they make you lean in?

---

**PASSAGE B -- Character Voice (Dialogue, tense exchange):**

> "You knew," she said. Not a question. A verdict.
>
> He looked at the window. "I knew what they told me."
>
> "That's not the same thing."

What to listen for: Can they differentiate two characters without going theatrical? Subtle shift in pitch/pace is good. Overdone accents are a fail.

## 3. Narrator Hiring Specs

### Platform
- **ACX (Audible's marketplace)** -- primary casting source
- Also check: Voices.com, Voice123, Backstage.com

### Search Filters on ACX
- Genre: Thriller / Mystery / True Crime / Suspense
- Voice style: Baritone, Gravelly, Dramatic
- Gender: Male (primary), open to deep female voice as alternate
- Experience: Minimum 3 completed audiobooks, 4.0+ listener rating

### Payment Options

| Option | Upfront Cost | Long-term Cost | Best If... |
|--------|-------------|----------------|------------|
| Royalty Share | $0 | 20% of ACX royalties for 7 years | Capital-constrained; testing market |
| Per Finished Hour (PFH) | $200-$400/hr x ~8 hrs = $1,600-$3,200 | $0 after payment | Expecting 320+ Audible sales (break-even) |
| Royalty Share + Fee | $100-$500 | 10-15% for shorter window | Compromise; established narrators |

**Recommendation:** Start with royalty share on first title. Use profits to go PFH on sequel once market is proven.

## 4. Narrator Direction Notes

Use these cues in session:

- "Slow it down by 20%" -- default speed is always too fast
- "You're telling me a secret" -- resets intimacy level
- "Let that sentence land before moving on" -- for punchy revelations
- "The character doesn't know they're being watched" -- for internal monologue
- "Morrison it" -- shorthand for slow, rising-inflection suspense build

### Technical Specs (Recording)
- Sample rate: 44.1 kHz, 16-bit, mono WAV (ACX requirement)
- Room noise floor: < -60 dBFS
- RMS range: -23 to -18 dBFS
- No plosives, mouth noise, or detectable reverb
- Mastered, de-noised before delivery

---

## 5. Quality Checklist (Before Approving Chapter)

- [ ] Narrator differentiation: at least 2 distinct characters have noticeably different delivery
- [ ] Pacing: no sentence feels rushed; silence is used as punctuation
- [ ] Consistency: same character sounds the same in Chapter 1 and Chapter 12
- [ ] No audible distractions: no clicks, breaths, traffic, chair squeaks
- [ ] ACX technical specs met
- [ ] "Morrison Test": Does the opening line make you want to keep listening?

---

## 6. Social Clip Voice Standard

For TikTok/IG Reels excerpts:

- Same narrator, same raw recording
- Clips: 60-90 seconds maximum
- Hook must land in first 5 seconds (scroll-stop equivalent)
- Add subtitles -- 85% of social video watched on mute
- No background music under narration (distracts from voice)
- End each clip with an open loop ("...and what happened next changed everything.")
