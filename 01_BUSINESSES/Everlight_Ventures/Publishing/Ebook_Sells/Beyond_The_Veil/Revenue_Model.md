## Narrator Cost Recovery

### Royalty Share vs Per Finished Hour

Assume 8-hour audiobook, 1,000 sales at $9.98 net/sale = $9,980 total audio royalties.

| Option | Upfront | 7-Year Royalty Cost | Total Cost | Net Profit (1K sales) |
|--------|---------|---------------------|------------|----------------------|
| Royalty Share (20% for 7 yrs) | $0 | $1,996 | $1,996 | $7,984 |
| PFH at $200/hr | $1,600 | $0 | $1,600 | $8,380 |
| PFH at $400/hr | $3,200 | $0 | $3,200 | $6,780 |

**Break-even for $200 PFH narrator:** 1,600 / 9.98 = ~161 sales
**Break-even for $400 PFH narrator:** 3,200 / 9.98 = ~321 sales

Royalty share wins if you sell fewer than ~250 copies. PFH wins beyond that.

## Key Levers That Move the Number

1. **Audible algorithm ranking** -- driven by reviews in the first 30 days. Ask every reader.
2. **TikTok clip virality** -- one clip hitting 500K+ views has historically 10x'd audiobook sales.
3. **Series multiplier** -- Book 2 nearly always outsells Book 1 once readers are hooked.
4. **Kindle Unlimited** -- pool payments add ~$0.005/page read. On a 300-page book at 10K reads/month = $150/month passive.
5. **Bundling** -- Ebook + Audiobook "bundle" perceived value is high. Not natively supported on Amazon but marketable via author website.
