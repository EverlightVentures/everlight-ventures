## PLAIN TEXT VERSION (for reference and social use)

She learned to leave her body to escape her bruises.
She did not know something was waiting for her on the other side.

Deputy Hailey Pink survives her days in Sable Flats one careful breath at
a time. The sheriff ignores her. Her boyfriend's fists speak louder than her
badge. The town she swore to protect is dying of something no doctor can name,
and nobody believes her.

But when the lights go out and she closes her eyes, she flies.

In the astral realm, she is untouchable. She swims through oceans of liquid
light, soars above floating islands of luminous forest, and moves like water
through dimensions her waking mind can barely hold. She is powerful there in
ways the frontier will never allow her to be.

Then the shadow finds her.

It does not speak. It does not threaten. It simply follows, patient and dark
and ancient, and the closer it comes, the more the people of Sable Flats begin
to hollow out, drifting through their days like shells of themselves, as if
something is feeding on the life they no longer have the will to protect.

The truth, when Hailey finally reaches it, is more devastating than any ghost
story.

The entity is not a monster. It is a man in another dimension who does not
even know he is doing it. His unresolved grief. His unhealed wounds. His
consciousness, leaking across the quantum web like a slow invisible poison,
bleeding into her world and killing everyone she loves.

To stop him, she will have to become something the frontier has never seen: a
warrior of the mind, a healer of broken dimensions, a woman willing to pay the
ultimate price.

"The real battle will be fought inside you."

For fans of The Midnight Library, Station Eleven, and The Dark Tower.
Comparable in spirit to Murakami, Cormac McCarthy, and Colleen Hoover.

**A World Unlike Any Western You Have Read:** Sable Flats is a dying frontier
town with a sickness no doctor can name, and Deputy Hailey Pink is the only
one paying attention. But the real story is happening somewhere else entirely,
in the luminous, terrifying space beyond sleep where Hailey has learned to go
when her waking life becomes too painful to inhabit.

**A Protagonist Who Earns Every Page:** Hailey Pink is twenty-two years old,
stubborn as the land she patrols, and quietly surviving an abuser who outranks
her in the social order of the West. She is not rescued. She rescues herself,
one astral projection at a time, and then turns around to rescue everyone else.
She is the kind of woman readers will want to follow across ten books.

**The Villain You Will Not See Coming:** The dark entity destroying Hailey's
town is not a demon, not a ghost, and not a force of nature. It is a man in
another dimension who is simply too broken to contain himself, his pain leaking
across the quantum web and poisoning a world he does not even know exists.
Understanding him does not make him less dangerous. It makes the story
devastating.

**Murakami Meets McCarthy Meets Colleen Hoover:** The prose shifts register
the way the story does: gritty and sparse in Sable Flats, lyrical and
expansive in the astral realm. The quantum physics underpinning the plot is
grounded entirely in feeling and metaphor, not textbook language. You do not
need to understand the science. You need to feel the connection. This book
trusts its readers to keep up.

**The Beginning of a Series Built to Last:** The Hailey Pink Chronicles are
designed as a trilogy. Book 1 ends with resolution and earned hope, not a
cliffhanger. But the final image, Hailey looking up at the sky and feeling
the quantum web still humming beneath everything, makes it impossible not to
want Book 2. The series grows with its protagonist. The frontier keeps
expanding. The web keeps pulling.

```
Book cover art. Cinematic, painterly, literary thriller aesthetic.

COMPOSITION: Full portrait orientation (6x9 inches at 300 DPI). A lone
female figure centered vertically in the lower two-thirds of the frame.

FIGURE: Silhouette of a woman in a long frontier duster coat and a
wide-brim hat. A sheriff's star badge is faintly visible at her chest,
catching a single point of light. She stands with quiet authority, not
posed or dramatic, simply present. Her edges dissolve upward into
particles of light and drifting stars, the dissolution beginning at her
shoulders and becoming complete at her crown. She is becoming something.
She is not afraid.

BACKGROUND: Split vertically, seamlessly blended at the center.

Left half: A dusty Western frontier town at dusk. Adobe and wood-frame
buildings. A wide empty main street. The light is amber and deep burnt
orange, long shadows, the last light before dark. The mood is quiet and
slightly wrong, a town holding its breath.

Right half: A deep cosmic astral realm. Dark blue-violet space. Luminous
quantum thread-lines stretching in every direction like a web of living
light. Floating landmasses covered in phosphorescent forest. An ocean of
liquid light far below. The feeling of enormous depth and ancient beauty.

COLOR PALETTE: Dusty amber, burnt sienna, and raw umber on the Western
side. Deep indigo, midnight blue, and soft violet on the astral side.
Where the two halves meet, they bleed into each other, purple-amber, the
exact color of a desert sunset at the moment the stars become visible.

ATMOSPHERE: Eerie and beautiful in equal measure. Literary. Evocative.
```

## "THE FIRST NIGHT SHE FLEW"
### A Prequel Story -- The Hailey Pink Chronicles

---

The first time it happened, she thought she was dying.

She had gone to bed with a bruise along her left side that bloomed purple
and green when she pressed it, a map of the night before, of Jake's
particular geography of anger. She had lain very still, the way she always
did afterward, making herself small and quiet and unremarkable in the dark,
waiting for her body to stop feeling like a crime scene.

She had not meant to leave it. She had not known it was possible.

It began as warmth.

Not the warmth of a body finding its way back to rest, but something else,
something that moved through her from the inside, threading between her bones
like light through a cracked shutter. She felt her breathing slow, then slow
again, beyond the ordinary rhythm of sleep, into something deeper and older,
a tempo she recognized but had never consciously heard before. As if some
part of her had been waiting her entire life for permission to breathe
this way.

Then the weight was gone.

Not gradually. Not the easing of a woman finally relaxing into sleep. Gone
the way a word you have been trying to remember simply surfaces, complete and
obvious and there. The ache in her ribs. The tightness across her shoulders
where she had braced for impact. The heaviness of the badge she always left
on the nightstand, the one that meant nothing to the man sleeping beside her.
All of it, simply gone.

She was standing in the room. But she was also lying in the bed. Both things
were true.

She looked down at her own hands and they were made of light.

She did not scream. She did not panic.

She looked at those hands of light and felt, for the first time in as long
as she could remember, completely safe.

And then she looked up, and the ceiling was gone, and the sky above Sable
Flats was full of stars she had never seen before, burning in colors with no
names, and she rose toward them like smoke from a fire that was finally
allowed to breathe.

She did not know yet what any of it meant.

She did not know about the shadow. She did not know about the sickness. She
did not know what the quantum web was, or that her consciousness was already
a thread within it, already entangled with something ancient and unresolved
in another world entirely.

She only knew that she was flying.

And that she was, for this one unguarded moment, free.
