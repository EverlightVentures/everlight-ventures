# Prologue: The Cost of Flight


There is no falling. There is only opening.

She feels it first as warmth, a warmth that does not touch her skin but passes through where skin used to be, settling in the spaces between atoms she no longer owns. Her body is somewhere far below, or far behind, or in a tense she has already forgotten. It does not matter. The weight of it, the bruises mapped across her ribs like dark continents, the badge pressing its star-shaped imprint into her chest, the gravity of a life spent bracing, all of it lifts. All of it goes.

And what remains is her.

Not her name. Not her face. Just the awareness that once lived inside those things, now unfolding like a hand releasing a held breath.

The quantum web receives her the way water receives ink. Slowly. Irreversibly. Thread by luminous thread, she spreads into the architecture of everything. She can feel it, the hum. Not a sound but a resonance, a single chord struck across every dimension at once, and she is inside the chord now, she is one of its notes. It vibrates through her consciousness and she understands, without language, that this hum has always been here. Before her. Before the town. Before the first star burned itself into being.

Colors bloom that she has no name for. They exist between the ones she knew, between violet and the frequency the eye was never built to catch. They taste of rain on copper. They feel like the moment before laughter, suspended.

She sees the threads.

They stretch in every direction, filaments of living light connecting soul to soul, thought to thought, reality to reality. She watches one thread pulse and knows, without knowing how she knows, that somewhere a child is being born. Another thread dims, and somewhere a man forgives himself for the first time in forty years. The web holds all of it. Every wound. Every wonder. Every small, stubborn act of love that no one else will ever see.

She is becoming part of this.

She had not known, when she first learned to fly, that this is what it would cost her.

But the strange thing, the thing that would frighten her if she still had a body capable of fear, is that she does not mind. The cost is enormous and the cost is nothing. She is losing everything she was and gaining everything that is. The equation balances. It has always balanced. She simply could not see it from the other side.

The last thread of her dissolves. There is no grief in it. There is no triumph. There is only the quiet truth of a woman who walked into the fire and found that the fire was made of light.

She smiles.

She lets go.

She becomes light.
