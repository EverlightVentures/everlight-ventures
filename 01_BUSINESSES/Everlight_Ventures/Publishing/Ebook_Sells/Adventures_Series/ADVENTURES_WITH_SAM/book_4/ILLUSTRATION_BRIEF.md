## OVERVIEW

This is Book 4 in a 5-book series. The visual language is already established. Your job is to honor what exists in Books 1-3 while giving Book 4 its own identity. The theme is environment and community responsibility. The palette is naturalistic and warm. The tone is hopeful without being naive.

The most important single image in this book is Spread 11: the heron walking into the water. Everything builds toward that moment. Every other spread should feel like it is heading there.

---

## COLOR PALETTE

**Primary palette for Book 4:**
- River blue (shifting throughout the book -- see notes below)
- Garden green (lush, earthy, not neon)
- Morning gold (early-morning warmth)
- Earth brown (soil, tree bark, fence wood)
- Warm grey (for murky water in early spreads)
- Sam's olive green shirt anchors each page

**The River's Visual Arc (critical):**
The river must visually change across the book. This change is the story told without words.

- Spreads 1-3: River is murky. Warm grey-green. A little dull. Unclear.
- Spreads 4-6: River still murky but Sam has noticed. No change yet.
- Spreads 8-10: River bank active with people working. Water still murky but the bank itself is cleaner.
- Spread 11 (BREAKTHROUGH): Water catches full afternoon light. Still not perfect, but something has shifted. The heron trusts it enough to step in.
- Spreads 12-13: River in warm evening light. Calmer. Less grey. Showing improvement.
- Spread 14: River through fence, golden evening. Alive-looking.

Do not make the Spread 11 river look perfectly clean -- that would be dishonest. Make it look like it is trying. That is the truth of the story.

## CHARACTER REFERENCE -- CANONICAL SPECS

### SAM
- Age: 5-7 (energetic, mid-age for the series arc)
- Skin: Warm, ambiguous (sun-touched, could be claimed by many backgrounds)
- Hair: Short, dark, messy (outside-kid hair, not styled)
- Eyes: Large, dark, expressive -- first thing you notice
- Expression default: Alert and curious, eyebrows slightly raised
- Posture: Rarely still. Motion lines encouraged.
- Clothing for Book 4: Olive green t-shirt with small wave graphic (book-specific). Well-worn jeans. Scuffed sneakers. The Backpack (see below).
- Flaw tells: Shoulders drop when disappointed (Spread 8). Impatience shows in forward-leaning posture.

**The Backpack:**
- Slightly too big for Sam
- Worn and patched in one corner
- A small family photo is visible at the outside pocket
- Unpacked only in the very last spread -- this is significant

### ROBO
- Compact robot, Sam's height or slightly shorter
- Rounded features throughout -- nothing sharp or threatening
- Chest display screen (PRIMARY EMOTIONAL INDICATOR): This is how Robo shows feelings. It must be legible and expressive in every spread.
- Non-threatening, rounded body -- designed to feel like a companion, not a machine
- No mouth. Eyes are simple circles or indicators.

**Robo's Display Screen Arc for Book 4:**
- Spread 2: Small downward graph (water quality declining)
- Spread 3: Simple downward arrow
- Spread 5: Arrow pointing outward (let's go)
- Spread 6: Clipboard with checkmarks accumulating
- Spread 7: Letters with long-vowel patterns highlighted
- Spread 8: Four stick figures, then more (possibility)
- Spread 9: Blank / processing (the quiet moment -- Robo does not know what to say)
- Spread 10: Clipboard, more names
- Spread 11: Small heron silhouette, GLOWING. This is his best moment. Make it beautiful.
- Spread 14: Small glowing heron (echoes Spread 11)

### MR. ADEYEMI (new character, must be drawn with care)
- Elderly, tall but stooped now with age
- Rich dark skin, weathered hands (hands of a man who has worked with them his whole life)
- Wide-brimmed gardening hat, gardening clothes
- Uses a hoe or fence for support -- not frail, but careful with himself
- Face: deeply warm. Has seen a lot. Trusts this kid.
- His photograph (Spread 4): Sepia-toned, shows a younger river, families picnicking, children swimming

### GRANDMA
- Warm, full-figured
- Natural grey hair (she does not hide it)
- Hands that look like they know things -- cooking hands, gardening hands
- Face: when she listens to Sam, her whole body turns toward him
- She appears only in Spread 13 -- her spread should feel like relief and arrival

### MR. RIVERA
- Stocky build, silver hair
- Uses a cane (important -- he is not embarrassed by it)
- His face when Sam gives him the lookout role: genuine gratitude, not just politeness
- On the bridge in Spread 10: waving someone over, in his element

### THE HERON (character, not background)
- Elegant. Patient. Dignified.
- NOT cartoony -- she is real in this world
- Long-legged, long-necked, grey-blue feathers
- Appears in Spreads 3, 8, 9, 10, 11, 14 (sometimes in background, always present at the water)
- Spread 11: Her walk into the water is slow and deliberate. One foot. Then the other. Neck bending. This is earned. Give it a full page if possible.

## SPREAD-BY-SPREAD NOTES

**Spread 1 (Opening -- double page, richly detailed):**
Sam at back fence, river behind him, morning gold. Hide these Easter eggs for re-readers:
- Heron far in background, standing still
- Plastic bag caught on branch over water
- Blank "RIVER WATCH" sign leaning against fence post
- Corner of family photo visible at Sam's backpack pocket
The illustration should reward two, three, four reads. Pack it.

**Spread 2 (Riverside path):**
Wide and open. Beautiful morning. The problem is there but subtle -- you have to look.
Robo's display shows a sun (good morning) on the left, then a downward graph on the right as Sam asks his question.

**Spread 3 (Close-up of bank -- the problem):**
Closer in. Slightly less warm light. Show what Sam sees: trash, murky water, the heron standing still. Her patience is visual stillness. She should be perfectly still.

**Spread 4 (Mr. Adeyemi):**
Full community garden visible behind him. The garden is lush and full -- what care looks like. The photograph he holds: show it clearly. Let the reader see the river in it. This spread should feel like time opening up.

**Spread 5 (The list):**
The handwritten list must feel real -- slightly uneven letters, some names in different inks. Sam taking it with both hands is important -- this is a handoff of responsibility.

**Spread 6 (Knocking on doors):**
Energy and humor. Could use a grid of small moments. The key beat: Sam's face when he looks at Mr. Rivera and his cane -- brief uncertainty -- then the new idea. Show that pivot.

**Spread 7 (Making flyers):**
The kitchen table should look lived-in. The flyers should look hand-made. Robo's display showing the phonics patterns should look like a simple visual -- not complicated. A few letters with the long vowel highlighted.

**Spread 8 (Four people):**
This spread should feel BIG and EMPTY. Wide shot. The river takes up most of the background. Sam looks small. Four people look smaller. The heron on the far bank, still waiting. Robo's display showing the growing stick figures is the only movement toward hope.

**Spread 9 (Mr. Adeyemi and Sam on the log):**
Quiet intimacy. Two figures on a log. Not much background action. Robo should be a little back, not part of this conversation -- watching, respectful. Robo's display: blank. Processing. Even Robo does not know what to say here.

**Spread 11 (THE HERON -- most important spread):**
Full double-page spread if possible. The heron in motion -- one foot in the water, then the other, neck bending. The cleanup group stands still watching. Nobody is moving. The afternoon light on the water. Sam's gloves off -- held in one hand. His face: not triumph. Awe. Something quieter than happiness.
Robo's display: the small heron icon, GLOWING. Warm white.
This spread is the reason the book exists.

**Spread 12 (River Watch sign):**
The garden extending to the path. Sam digging with both hands (dirty). The sign on the fence plank -- hand-lettered, two names so far, room for more. The garden touching the river. This is what hopeful looks like.

**Spread 13 (Grandma):**
Last of the evening light. Golden. Long shadows. Sam's hands are animated as he talks. Grandma is completely still -- listening. The river visible through the fence in the background, catching the gold. This is a slow, warm spread.

**Spread 14 (Closing):**
Sam in the doorway. The backpack UNPACKED on the floor -- this matters. Lay out its contents clearly: notebook, flashlight, string, family photo. The RIVER WATCH notebook on top, open to a new page. Robo beside him. River through the fence. The final image should feel like the beginning of something.

- Grandma's grey hair and listening posture
