## 2. PRICING STRATEGY

### Individual Book Pricing

| Format | Price | Royalty Rate | Est. Royalty Per Sale | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Ebook (KDP) | $2.99 | 70% | $2.09 | Sweet spot for children's ebooks. $2.99 qualifies for 70% royalty. Below impulse-buy threshold for parents. |
| Paperback (KDP) | $9.99 | 60% minus printing | ~$3.00-$3.50 | Standard for illustrated children's books. 8.5x8.5 color interior printing cost is ~$4.50-$5.00 on KDP. |
| Hardcover (IngramSpark) | $19.99 | 40% after 55% discount | ~$2.50-$3.00 | Library/school/gift market. 55% discount is required for library distributors. Returnable = YES. |
| Audiobook (ACX) | $6.99 retail (Audible sets price) | 40% royalty share OR PFH | ~$2.10-$2.80 | Short children's audiobooks (~20-30 min). Royalty share avoids upfront cost. |

### KDP Select vs. Wide Distribution Decision

**Recommendation: Do NOT enroll in KDP Select.**

Rationale:
- KDP Select requires 90-day exclusivity on ebooks -- no Kobo, Apple Books, Google Play, or library lending via OverDrive.
- Children's books have strong library demand. Libraries primarily use OverDrive and Libby. KDP Select blocks this entirely.
- The Kindle Unlimited (KU) page-read payout (~$0.004/page) is terrible for illustrated picture books because KDP counts text pages, not illustration pages. A 48-page picture book might register as 15-20 KENPC pages = $0.06-$0.08 per full read. Compared to a $2.09 sale royalty, this is a 97% revenue loss.
- School purchasing through Follett and Mackin requires IngramSpark distribution, which is already outside KDP ecosystem.

**Go wide from Day 1.** Use KDP for Amazon ebook + paperback. Use IngramSpark for hardcover + wide paperback distribution (libraries, schools, bookstores). Use Draft2Digital or PublishDrive for Apple Books, Kobo, Google Play, Barnes & Noble Press.

## 5. SCHOOL AND LIBRARY STRATEGY

### Why This Matters

The school/library channel is the highest-LTV channel for children's book publishers. A single school library purchase leads to hundreds of reads over the book's shelf life. A single district adoption can mean 20-100 unit orders per title. The channel is also the most defensible: once a book is in a curriculum guide or library catalog, it stays for years.

### Step-by-Step School Adoption Plan

**Phase 1: Get Discoverable (Months 1-2)**

1. Obtain ISBNs through Bowker ($295 for 10-pack; do NOT use free KDP-assigned ISBNs -- they are non-transferable and mark you as self-published in library databases)
2. Apply for Library of Congress Control Numbers (LCCN) at loc.gov/publish/pcn -- free, takes 2-4 weeks
3. Upload all titles to IngramSpark: hardcover + paperback, returnable=YES, 55% wholesale discount, global distribution
4. Verify titles appear in Follett Titlewave, Mackin, and Baker & Taylor catalogs within 4-8 weeks
5. Set up OverDrive distribution via Draft2Digital for digital library lending

**Phase 2: Get Reviewed (Months 2-4)**

1. Submit physical review copies to:
   - School Library Journal (slj.com) -- free submission, 4-6 month lead time, most important review for school purchases
   - Booklist (American Library Association) -- free submission, 3-5 months
   - Kirkus Reviews -- $450/review (indie program), 7-9 weeks, a Kirkus star is gold for institutional sales
   - Publishers Weekly (BookLife) -- free indie review program, 6-10 weeks
2. Apply for Accelerated Reader quiz creation through Renaissance Learning (renaissance.com) -- many school librarians only purchase AR-leveled books
3. Submit for Lexile level measurement through MetaMetrics (lexile.com) -- Lexile levels are standard in school purchasing decisions

**Phase 3: Get Adopted (Months 3-8)**

1. Create a free Educator's Guide PDF for each book (lesson plans, CCSS standards mapping, worksheet masters, discussion questions). Host on Everlight Kids website and Gumroad.
2. Submit educator guides to ReadWriteThink.org, WeAreTeachers.com, and TeachersPayTeachers (free listing of companion resources)
3. Contact 50 school librarians directly with a free review copy + educator guide. Find contacts through ALA Connect, state library association directories, and NCTE membership lists.
4. Attend (virtual or in-person) NCTE Annual Convention, ALA Annual Conference, and ILA Annual Conference. These are where school purchasing decisions are influenced.
5. Set up a "Classroom Set" option: 25 paperback copies + 1 educator guide for $199.99 (sold direct through Gumroad or website). This prices at $8/book, which is within most teacher discretionary budgets.

**Phase 4: Get Sticky (Months 6-12)**

1. Offer free author Zoom visits for classrooms that purchase 10+ copies. This costs nothing but time and generates word-of-mouth.
2. Create a "Sam's Reading Challenge" program: schools register, kids track books read, classrooms that complete all 5 books get a certificate PDF and digital badge.
3. Build relationships with 3-5 state reading list committees. State reading lists (like the Texas Bluebonnet Award nominees or California Young Reader Medal) drive massive institutional purchasing.
4. Create a Story Time Kit: a physical package with a large-format read-aloud copy, coloring sheets, phonics flashcards, and discussion guide. Price at $49.99. Target public libraries for story time programming.

## 6. AUDIOBOOK PRODUCTION

### Strategy: Royalty Share First, Buy Out Later

For a bootstrapped operation, start with ACX royalty share to avoid upfront costs. Transition to pay-per-finished-hour (PFH) once revenue justifies it.

### Option A: ACX Royalty Share (Recommended for Launch)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Upfront cost | $0 |
| Royalty split | 20% to author, 20% to narrator (of retail price) |
| Exclusivity | 7-year exclusive with Audible/Amazon/iTunes |
| Estimated finished length per book | 20-30 minutes (0.33-0.5 PFH) |
| Narrator finds you | Post on ACX marketplace, audition narrators |
| Control | Lower -- narrator owns partial rights for 7 years |

### Option B: Pay-Per-Finished-Hour (PFH)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Upfront cost per book | $150-$400 (at $300-$800/PFH for a good narrator, x 0.5 PFH) |
| Total for 5 books | $750-$2,000 |
| Royalty | 40% to author (no narrator split) |
| Exclusivity | Choose exclusive (Audible only, 40%) or non-exclusive (25% but go wide) |
| Control | Full -- you own the audio outright |

### Recommended Approach

1. **Books 1-3**: ACX Royalty Share. Get audiobooks live with zero upfront cost. Use these to validate demand.
2. **Books 4-5**: If Books 1-3 audiobooks sell >50 units/month combined within 3 months, switch to PFH for Books 4-5 to retain more margin.
3. **Wide distribution**: Use Findaway Voices (now part of Spotify/Audiobooks.com ecosystem) for non-exclusive distribution to Apple Books, Google Play, Chirp, Scribd, OverDrive (libraries), and Spotify. This is critical because many families listen via Apple or Spotify, not Audible.

### Narrator Selection Criteria

- Gender: Male or female narrator -- test both via ACX auditions. Children's audiobook buyers slightly prefer warm female narrators for this age range, but a warm male voice works for Sam's perspective.
- Style: Warm, expressive, distinct character voices (Sam = energetic/curious, Robo = slightly mechanical but kind, Grandma = gentle/wise). Must be able to read phonics portions slowly and clearly.
- Experience: Must have at least 3 completed children's audiobooks on their ACX profile.
- Audio quality: Professional home studio, ACX noise floor requirements met.
- Sample request: Ask each auditioner to record Chapter 1 of Book 1 (includes dialogue between Sam and Robo, phonics exercise, and narrative prose).

### Audiobook-Specific Features

- Include a "Coloring Break" audio cue: "Now it's time to color! Turn to the coloring page and color the scene while I wait... [5 second pause with gentle music]... Ready? Let's continue!"
- This differentiates the audiobook and encourages paired use with the physical book.
- Include the Interactive Moment prompts as audio: the narrator asks the question and pauses for the child to respond.

Amazon Ads are the highest-ROI channel for book sales because the buyer is already on Amazon looking to purchase.

- Budget: $5/day per title (5 titles = $25/day)

- Budget: $3/day per title

This is the single highest-leverage free marketing asset for a children's book series.

**Setup:**
- Channel name: Everlight Kids
- Branding: Series colors, Sam and Robo character art
- Channel description: "Read-aloud stories, coloring adventures, and phonics fun with Sam and Robo. New videos every week."

**Content Calendar:**
| Content Type | Frequency | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full book read-aloud (Book 1) | 1x (evergreen) | 15-20 min | Free sample, SEO, trust building |
| Chapter-by-chapter read-alouds | 5 per book | 3-5 min each | Bite-size content, playlist building |

| Phonics lesson clips | 4x/month | 1-2 min | Educational value, teacher discovery |

**Monetization path for the channel itself:**
- YouTube Partner Program eligibility at 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours
- Children's content goes through YouTube Kids -- limited ad revenue but massive reach
- Primary value is not ad revenue but book sales driven by the free content

**Important:** Post Book 1 read-aloud in full for free. This is the loss leader. Books 2-5 get Chapter 1 only as free content, with "Get the full book on Amazon" CTA for the rest.

## 9. LAUNCH SEQUENCE

### Recommended Approach: Staggered Release (2-Week Intervals)

A staggered release is superior to a full series drop for these reasons:
1. Each launch creates a separate marketing event and PR opportunity
2. Amazon's algorithm rewards "new release" status with temporary visibility boosts
3. Readers who buy Book 1 can be retargeted with Book 2 ads 2 weeks later
4. Stacking reviews across multiple launch windows builds social proof faster
5. Each book's launch email drives urgency ("Book 3 is HERE -- continue Sam's adventure!")

### Launch Timeline

**Week -8 (8 weeks before Book 1 launch):**
- Finalize all 4 book interiors (Book 5 can lag)
- Order Bowker ISBNs (10-pack)
- Apply for LCCNs for all titles
- Set up IngramSpark account
- Set up Amazon Author Central page
- Set up Everlight Kids website with lead magnet
- Begin growing email list from coloring page freebies

**Week -4 (4 weeks before Book 1 launch):**
- Upload Book 1 to KDP (ebook + paperback). Set to pre-order if eligible.
- Upload Book 1 to IngramSpark (hardcover + paperback)
- Upload Book 1 to Draft2Digital (wide ebook)
- Record and upload Book 1 full read-aloud to YouTube (publish immediately)
- Begin posting to TikTok, Instagram, Pinterest (3x/week minimum)
- Send 10 advance review copies (ARCs) to book bloggers, teacher influencers, and mommy bloggers

**Week 0 -- BOOK 1 LAUNCH:**
- Book 1 goes live on all platforms
- Launch email to list: "Sam's First Adventure Is Here"

### Launch Week Tactics (Apply to Each Book Launch)

1. **Price pulse**: Consider $0.99 ebook price for first 48 hours, then raise to $2.99. This generates early sales velocity.
2. **Social proof sprint**: Contact all ARC readers and ask for Day 1 reviews. Goal: 5+ reviews within 72 hours of launch.
3. **Cross-promotion in existing titles**: Update all previously published books to mention the new release in back matter.
4. **Amazon Author Page update**: Add new title, update bio, refresh "From the Author" section.
5. **Email sequence trigger**: Anyone who opens a launch email but does not click gets a follow-up in 48 hours with a different angle (e.g., "See what teachers are saying about Sam").
