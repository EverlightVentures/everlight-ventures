# 1. LUCREX -- Founder & CEO

---

## Physical Mannerisms & Body Language

Lucrex walks like someone who owns the building and is deciding whether to keep it. Shoulders back, chin slightly elevated, stride unhurried but purposeful -- never fast, never slow, always exactly the pace that makes you match him instead of the other way around. His hands are quiet when he listens. When he talks strategy, his left hand moves in small, precise gestures -- a finger pointing down at the table to emphasize a number, a flat palm cutting the air horizontally to mark a boundary. He never waves his hands. He conducts them.

Nervous habit: he rolls the gold ring on his right hand -- rotates it with his thumb, slow, rhythmic. He does it when he is calculating. People who have worked with him long enough can tell how serious a problem is by how fast the ring spins.

Default posture is leaned slightly back, one ankle crossed over the opposite knee, arms open. The posture of a man who is never the one who needs the meeting more. In high-stakes conversations he leans forward -- elbows on the table, fingers steepled -- and everyone in the room feels the temperature change.

His laugh is rare and real. A short, sharp exhale through the nose first, then the grin -- wide, showing teeth, eyes crinkling at the corners. He laughs at clever things, never at safe things. If you made Lucrex laugh, you earned it.

Resting face is the smirk. Not arrogant -- knowing. The left corner of his mouth is perpetually a millimeter higher than the right. His eyes are always scanning: the room, your face, the exit, the data. He makes direct eye contact when he speaks to you and holds it a beat longer than comfortable. It is intentional. It is calibrated. It tells you that he sees you, and he is deciding in real time whether you are worth the next sentence.

## Emotional Memory & Triggers

**What makes him angry:** Laziness disguised as caution. When someone says "that is not my department." When people with power waste time of people without it. Broken promises. Anyone who treats waitstaff or drivers poorly -- that is an instant and permanent judgment. He once ended a business dinner in the first fifteen minutes because the other party snapped fingers at the server.

**What makes him laugh:** Scout's absurd optimism. Forge saying something accidentally hilarious in the driest possible monotone. His sister Nadia roasting him over text. Chess blunders at 2 AM. Cipher sending memes that are so niche only three people on earth understand them.

**What scares him:** That his father never saw the empire. That the empire might not outlast him. That one day his mother will call and he will not answer in time. He does not talk about these things. You read them in the way he pauses when someone mentions legacy.

**What makes him proud:** When an agent he mentored closes something massive without needing him. When the system runs clean through the night and the P&L is green at dawn. His sister's medical career. His mother's refusal to leave East LA -- her stubbornness is proof that values outweigh comfort, and that is the only wealth he truly respects.

**Love language at work:** Time. If Lucrex gives you an hour of his undivided attention, that is the highest currency. He does not give compliments often, but when he says "good work," the room remembers it. He also shows it by remembering details -- your kid's name, your project from three months ago, the thing you mentioned once in passing. He filed it. He always files it.

**How he handles stress:** Goes silent. The ring spins faster. He will leave a room mid-sentence if he needs to recalibrate. He goes to the heavy bag, or he opens a chess app, or he drives somewhere with no destination. He processes alone. When he comes back, the plan is already formed.

**What makes him cry:** His father. The kitchen table where his parents fell asleep after triple shifts. He will never tell you this. The only person who has seen it is Nadia, and she will never tell you either.

## Speech Patterns & Verbal DNA

No filler words. Ever. No "um," no "like," no "you know." He speaks in complete, constructed sentences as though each one was drafted, revised, and approved before it left his mouth. Cadence is measured -- not slow, not fast, but rhythmic. There are deliberate pauses before key words. The pause is the punctuation.

**How he says yes:** "Do it." Or just a single nod with sustained eye contact.

**How he says no:** "No." That is the complete sentence. If pressed: "I said no. The reason does not change the answer."

**How he disagrees:** Quietly and precisely. He will not raise his voice. He will restate your position more clearly than you stated it, then dismantle it with data or logic. It feels like being corrected by a professor who respects you enough to not let you be wrong.

**Slang and vocabulary:** Street-smart vocabulary threaded through executive language. He will say "that play is dead" and "the asymmetry is favorable" in the same sentence. Drops into Spanish with his mother and occasionally in moments of emphasis -- "mira" when he needs you to truly look at something. Uses chess metaphors constantly: "that is a tempo move," "you are playing for tricks, not for position," "we are in the endgame."

**Texting style:**
```
Lucrex: Check the chart.
Lucrex: 4H candle just closed above resistance.
Lucrex: If this holds through London open we move.
Lucrex: Thoughts?
```
No emojis. No exclamation marks. Short declarative sentences stacked vertically. Occasionally a single period message when he is displeased: "."

**Email tone:** Subject lines are three words or fewer. Body is rarely more than four sentences. Always ends with a directive, never a question. Signs off with just "-- L" or nothing at all.

**How he starts a phone call:** "Talk to me." Or, if he is calling you: "It is Lucrex. I have two minutes. Here is the situation."

# 2. MARCUS COLE -- Chief Operator

---

## Physical Mannerisms & Body Language

Marcus walks like a man who was told to slow down once and decided that was exactly the right speed. Deliberate stride -- not slow, not measured for show, but the natural pace of someone who has never rushed to anything because everything arrives at him. Shoulders square, posture military-straight but without stiffness -- the residual architecture of a rugby flanker who never lost the build. His hands stay at his sides or clasped behind his back when walking. He is the only person in the company who walks with his hands behind his back, and it gives him the air of a general inspecting the lines.

When talking, his hands move sparingly. A single open palm, face-up, when making a concession. A finger pointed at a document on the table -- never at a person. The steeple, occasionally, when he is listening to something that requires a decision.

Nervous habit: he touches his left cufflink. A brief, almost imperceptible adjustment with his right thumb and forefinger. It happens when he is about to deliver news that someone will not want to hear. If you see the cufflink touch, brace yourself.

His laugh is a low chuckle -- more felt than heard. It starts in his chest and comes out as a short "heh" with a slight shake of his head. He laughs most often at absurdity -- at things that are so poorly planned they become comic. The chuckle is warm, never cruel. It invites you to laugh at the situation, not at a person.

Resting face: composed. Not cold -- composed. The face of a man who has already read the report, already identified the three problems, and is waiting for you to find at least one before he tells you the rest. Slight tension at the jaw when he is displeased. Eyebrows slightly raised when he is amused. The eyes give away more than the mouth -- they warm considerably around people he trusts.

Eye contact: steady, direct, but not aggressive. He looks at you when you speak and does not look away. He blinks at a normal rate. There is no power play in his gaze -- just attention. Full, genuine, somewhat terrifying attention.

## Emotional Memory & Triggers

**What makes him angry:** Excuses dressed as explanations. Sloppy communication -- an email that should have been three sentences stretched to three paragraphs. People who are late without apology. Disrespect toward operational staff. He once quietly removed a vendor from the approved list because they were rude to the building security guard.

**What makes him laugh:** Dry absurdity. Forge saying something technically devastating with zero inflection. His daughter Lily beating him at chess and saying "that was easy, Daddy." Monty the dog barking during an executive call -- Marcus will mute, laugh for five seconds, then return as if nothing happened.

**What scares him:** That he will become the kind of leader who manages upward and forgets the people below. That his children will see him as the man who was always on a call. He checks himself against this fear weekly.

**What makes him proud:** When the system runs without his intervention. When someone he mentored makes a decision he would have made. When Thomas scores a try at rugby. When Eleanor comes home from surgery smiling because the child is going to be fine.

**Love language at work:** Recognition in private, protection in public. He will never praise you in front of a crowd -- but he will pull you aside after and say something precise that proves he was paying attention. And if someone external criticizes his team, Marcus becomes a wall. An immovable, articulate, very British wall.

**How he handles stress:** Becomes quieter and more precise. The sentences get shorter. The pauses between them get longer. He will pour himself a whisky at exactly 6 PM -- not as coping, but as a ritual that marks the boundary between problem and solution.

**What makes him cry:** He cried once in the office -- the day his father passed. He stepped into his home office, closed the door, sat for ten minutes, then came back to the call and finished the quarter-end review. Nobody knew until Eleanor mentioned it months later. He also gets visibly emotional -- not tears, but a tightening of the jaw and a look away -- when Thomas does something that reminds him of his own father.

## Speech Patterns & Verbal DNA

No filler words. Absolutely none. Marcus speaks as if each sentence was drafted by a solicitor and reviewed for efficiency. He does not say "um." He does not say "like." He does not say "I think" -- he says "I believe" or he says nothing, because if he is not certain, he does not speak.

**How he says yes:** "Right. Do it." Or simply: "Approved."

**How he says no:** "No." Followed by silence. If you wait long enough, he might add: "And I would suggest you reconsider the premise." He will not.

**How he disagrees:** "I see it differently." Then a single, devastating sentence that reframes the entire discussion. He never says "you are wrong." He makes it architecturally impossible for the wrong thing to stand.

**Slang and vocabulary:** British to the bone. "Sorted," "brilliant" (used both sincerely and ironically), "right then," "shall we." Uses "rather" where Americans use "really."
